The problem

Every organisation runs on people asking people.

A new starter asks their manager. A technician asks the senior engineer. A branch asks head office. The same questions, asked again and again — answered from memory, by the same few people.

Those people are your most experienced. Which means your organisation’s capacity to grow is capped by the calendar of whoever knows the most. Knowledge held in people doesn’t scale. It queues.

The hidden cost

The cost never appears on an invoice.

Knowledge dependency doesn’t fail loudly. It accumulates quietly, in four places at once.

Onboarding drag

New hires take months to become independent — not because they can’t learn, but because the answers they need live in other people’s heads and other people’s time.

Interrupted experts

Your most valuable people spend their days answering questions they’ve answered a hundred times before, instead of doing the work only they can do.

Inconsistent answers

Ask three people, get three answers. Every variation becomes a different process, a different standard, a different risk.

Knowledge that walks out the door

When an experienced person leaves, everything they knew that was never written down leaves with them. Permanently.

more people→ more questions

more interruptions→ more friction
more friction→ slower growth

The bottleneck compounds as you grow. Mikora breaks the chain at the second line.

Questions

Asked before you ask.

Is Mikora a chatbot?

Chat is one of the ways people reach Mikora — usually inside tools they already use, such as WhatsApp or internal messaging. The product is what sits underneath: a structured organisational memory, escalation logic that routes risk to humans, and an intelligence layer that shows leaders how knowledge is moving through the business. The conversation is the access point, not the system.

Where do Mikora’s answers come from?

From your organisation — your procedures, policies, training material and expert input, reviewed and approved before they are used. Mikora doesn’t answer from the open internet, and your knowledge isn’t shared beyond your organisation.

What happens when a question is high-risk or uncertain?

It is escalated immediately to the person your organisation has designated, with the full context of the conversation attached — rather than answered automatically. Escalation paths are configured around your structure and your standards.

How is Mikora set up?

Each deployment is configured around your organisation: your knowledge sources, communication standards, escalation pathways and reporting requirements. You don’t adapt to Mikora — it is shaped to how you already operate.

Which industries does Mikora serve?

Any organisation that runs on expertise and consistency — from healthcare and manufacturing to franchise networks, field operations and professional services. The platform is industry-agnostic; the configuration is specific to you.

What does Mikora replace?

Usually nothing. It sits alongside your existing systems as a layer between your people and what your organisation knows — reducing the load on your experts, not the tools you’ve already invested in.
